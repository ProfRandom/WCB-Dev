### Additions from *Approximating Season Lengths*
- **Sinusoidal Approximation**  
  Approximate method for calculating seasonal lengths using a sine-wave fit to declination.  

- **Kepler‚Äôs Exact Method**  
  Precise method for calculating seasonal lengths using Kepler‚Äôs Second Law and elliptical orbital geometry.  

- **SANC** (*Simple, Approximate, Notationally Clear*)  
  Guideline prioritizing clarity in approximations when exact math is unnecessarily complex.  

- **Generalized Fudge Factor (f = 10e)**  
  Adjustment factor used in approximating seasonal lengths, scaling eccentricity effects.  

- **Sidereal chronum (C)**  
  Orbital period of a planemo relative to the inertial frame; distinguished from apical chronum.  


### Additions from *Calculating Stellar Sky Paths*
- **Azimem (Z)**  
  Azimuthal intersection point of a stellar/solar path with the horizon.  
  - **East Azimem ($Z_e$):** intersection on the eastern horizon.  
  - **West Azimem ($Z_w$):** intersection on the western horizon.  

- **Azimuthem**  
  Great-circle arc through zenith connecting east to west; always 180¬∞.  

- **Altitudem**  
  Altitude of ellipse center relative to horizon.  

- **Obliquem**  
  Half of the planet‚Äôs axial obliquity ($\varepsilon_x/2$).  

**Canon Note**: In WCB usage, the older ellipse parameters (semi-major and semi-minor axes) are deprecated for sky-path geometry. They are replaced by **Azimuthem** (horizontal span = 180¬∞) and **Obliquem** (vertical span = half obliquity).
